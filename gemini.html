This project is dedicated to Ella Music and Julia Borges de Villasante.

Two kids at ABC approached me one day and asked if I could make a MoonBoard app where an AI generates climbs in certain grades taking the climber’s reach into consideration. I’ve already discussed this idea with a few colleagues, but the kids asking nudged me a little farther.

Coming up with AI-generated board climbs is easy. However, what we need is parameterization. In other words, we need an AI that can generate climbs given certain parameters, reach being our parameter in Ella and Julia’s case. Sure, you can easily take grades into consideration because the dataset is right there. But how about reach, age, or even style of climbing? This is a much harder problem because there is a serious lack of data labeling in most cases and no easy way to label them.

Going into it, I realized that a more mathematically rigorous system that contains our prior knowledge about all these parameters may do better than an end-to-end generative AI. For the next few days, I kept thinking. One night, I had an idea.

Developing on that idea, I realized this very idea may be a good foundation for representing any climbs on any wall mathematically. The model is still in its infancy, and I believe focusing on the board climbs will properly develop the idea for harder projects.

The posts for this project will contain topics ranging from the system itself all the way to random crazy ideas I might have. Enjoy!
